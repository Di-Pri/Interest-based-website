Nusa Islands

Three tropical islands with pristine white-sand beaches just off the Bali coast, Nusa Penida, Lembongan and Ceningan, are popular yet relatively quiet islands that have to be on your Bali itinerary. An icon for Lembongan and Ceningan is the Yellow suspension bridge connecting them. Nusa Penida is the biggest of the three islands, but until now, the least developed. By visiting Nusa Penida expect turquoise and crystal clear water, 200-meter high coastal cliffs drop, and yet untouched nature.

Places to see in Nusa Islands

Kelingking beach

Kelingking beach is the most spectacular place on Nusa Penida. White sand and a piece of land shaped like a dinosaur head. From the viewpoint, you have a look over the limestone formations, ocean, and Kelingking Beach.

Angel's Billabong

The second popular thing to do in Nusa Penida is visiting the natural infinity pool named Angel's Billabong. Take a dip in the super clear water. Be aware to not go too near the edge of the pool since the sea gets over and creates dangerous situations.

Broken Beach

Despite the name, there isn't an actual beach here. You will witness one gorgeous bay with an archway opening at the end. The waves from the ocean have been causing erosion to the cliff for centuries and create the archway that influenced the name "Broken Beach".

Devil's Tear

Experience the powerful waves crashing into the cliffsides on Lembongan islands. Devil's Tear is an impressive, somewhat dangerous, blowhole where the water can easily reach meters-high into the air.
